Anti Bullying

What is bullying?

Bullying is the act of repeating an action with the intention of harming someone emotionally or physically. There are many types of bullying the four main types are: Physical, Verbal, social and cyber.

What is verbal bullying?

Verbal bullying is the act of bullying with words. Verbal bullying can be like name calling people or hurtful words to people with disabilities. Everyone can think of examples but don’t say it to people for obvious reasons.

What counts as physical bullying?

Physical bullying is repeated physical alterations and actions to harm and to scare people for malicious use.

Social bullying

Social bullying can be excluded from people. This can be like excluding them from games, Parties, social outing and other things or talking bad about them.

Cyber bullying

Cyberbullying is the act of people repeatedly making fun of people online for an extended period of time. This can be making posts about the person or even just texting the person. This is a prominent version of bullying.

What should you do if your getting bullied?

If you are getting bullied, you need to tell a trusted family member, and the school can be a teacher or any trusted adult in school. If you are getting bullied outside of school or online, you must tell an adult. If it's online, you can block the bully online on many socials and also tell an adult. Remember always tell an adult.

Who should you tell within our school?

Within our school you can tell any teacher but mainly any teacher you feel comfortable to talk to. If you feel lost or confused or need to talk to someone you can go to your year head, a guidance counselor or your base class teacher.
